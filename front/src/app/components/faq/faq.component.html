Frequently Asked Questions

What is a password manager?

A password manager is a software application designed to store and manage online credentials. It securely stores your passwords and helps you log into your online accounts automatically.

How does the password manager keep my data secure?

Our password manager uses advanced encryption algorithms to secure your data. Your Master Password is the key to decrypting your data, ensuring that only you can access your sensitive information.

What is a master password? Visit our dedicated page about master password
What should I do if I forget my Master Password?

If you forget your Master Password, unfortunately, there is no way to recover it. This is because your data is encrypted with your Master Password, and without it, the data cannot be decrypted. It's crucial to remember your Master Password or store it in a safe place.

Why does the application ask me to re-enter my master password when I refresh the page?

For security reasons, your Master Password is only stored temporarily in your browser's memory. When you refresh the page, this memory is cleared, so you need to enter your Master Password again to access your data.

Is it safe to store all my passwords in one place?

Yes, it is safe to store all your passwords in one place with a password manager. Password managers are designed with security as a top priority, using strong encryption to protect your data from unauthorized access.

What happens if I delete a vault ?

Vault deletion is a permanent action. Once you delete a vault, all the logins associated will be sent to the recycle bin. You can restore them later if needed.

What is an Orphaned Login? (in recycle bin)

An Orphaned Login is a login that is not associated with any vault. This can happen if the vault it was associated with has been deleted. Orphaned Logins can be restored or permanently deleted from the recycle bin.
